Cyber Ethics_

Cyber ethics, also known as internet ethics or digital ethics, refers to the ethical principles and guidelines that govern the use of technology, particularly in the online realm. As our dependence on digital technologies continues to grow, issues related to privacy, security, and responsible online behavior have become increasingly significant. Cyber ethics seeks to address these concerns and establish a framework for ethical conduct in the digital space.

Privacy is how companies collect, manage, store, and control the use of data. Cybersecurity involves securing data from unauthorized use or access. Data privacy is the protection of personal data from those who should not have access to it. It also refers to the ability of individuals to determine who can access their personal information. Data privacy focuses on issues related to collecting, storing, and retaining data, as well as data transfers within applicable regulations and laws.

Some examples of data privacy include:

  • Ensuring that sensitive data, such as financial information or medical records, is only accessed by authorized personnel
  • Access control measures, such as usernames and passwords, or biometric authentication
  • Encrypting data
  • install Privacy software

Digital rights are human rights and legal rights that allow people to access, use, create, and publish digital media. They also allow people to access and use computers, other electronic devices, and telecommunications networks. Digital rights are closely linked to freedom of expression and privacy. They are an extension of human rights for the internet age.

Some examples of digital rights include:

  1. The right to access and use computers and/or other electronic devices
  2. The right to access and use digital content
  3. The right to create and share digital media
  4. The right to privacy in digital communities

cyber crime is different from any other crime happening in the society. The cyber crime define to criminal activity in which computers or computer networks are a tool a target or a place of criminal activity and include everything from electronic wracking to denial of services attacks. It's a general term that covers crimes like phishing, credit card frauds, bank robbery, illegal downloading, child pornography, kidnapping children via chat rooms, scams, cyber terrorism, and distribution of viruses etc.

Some examples of cybercrime include:

  • Phishing: A technique that uses deceptive emails, websites, and text messages to steal confidential information.
  • Ransomware: A type of malicious software that prevents you from accessing your computer files, systems, or networks and demands you pay a ransom for their return.
  • Identity theft: When someone uses another person's personal information, like their Social Security number, to commit fraud or other crimes.
  • DDoS attacks: Distributed Denial of Service attacks that are programmed to overwhelm a network or website with traffic, causing it to slow down or crash entirely.

The Indian Penal Code also contains several sections related to cybercrime. Sections 406, 408, 420, and 468 of the IPC deal with offenses related to online fraud and cheating. Section 509 deals with offenses related to online harassment and stalking.
